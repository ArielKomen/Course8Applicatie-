Help page

What is a bitter melon?

  The bitter gourd (Momordica charantia) is a fruit/vegetable known for its extremely bitter taste, as well as its many health benefits.

What are the different tabs?

  On the home page you will find the sunburst diagram and buttons to interact with. On this page you will find some text that might help you navigate. On the disclaimer page you will find some disclaimers regarding our application and the database it interacts with. On the about page you will find some information about this projects and the people that worked on it.

What does the sunburst display?

  The sunburst on our the home page displays pubmed articles. The inner circle represents the bittermelon, our vegetable/fruit of choice. You can change the meaning of the second circle to either diseases or compounds. If you choose for the second circle to be diseases the third circle will stand for the compounds. When you click on the disease you're interested in the sunburst will show what compounds are in the pubmed articles that are about bitter gourd and that specific disease.

How do I interact with the sunburst?

  To interact with our sunburst you first select what you want the second and third circle to mean with the 2 buttons on the left. After that you click on the disease/compound in the sunburst that you're interested in in the second circle of the sunburst. At last you click on the disease/compound that you're interested in in the third circle of the sunburst.

How do I interact with the menu on the left?

  The big menu on the left is a list of diseases/compounds (depending on which one you click in the smaller menu). When you click on one of the names, a second list appears. Behind the names of the compounds/diseases you will find the number of articles that has both terms in it. When you have selected both a disease and a compound you can scroll down to find a table which displays all of the articles containing these 2 terms.

How do I interact with the table of articles?

  When you have completed the steps above and the table is filled with all the articles that you would like to take a closer look at, we will move on to the next step. You can select, with the checkboxes, the articles you would like to download. When you click on the submit button a file with these articles (title, date, pubmedID and hyperlink) will be downloaded onto your computer. If you do not want to download anything, it will be usefull to know that the links in this table are already clickable!

How do I download the articles that I am interested in?

  If you selected a few articles with the steps above, you can click on the Download button. You will see a page with the articles that you selected. Once you see this page you can click on your right mouse button and click the option "Downloa page as...". The page with your selected articles will be transported to your download map on your computer. To go back and explore some more you will need to click the <- button at the top left of your screen(outside of the application).
